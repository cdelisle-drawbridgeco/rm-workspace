The Risk Landscape

At most firms, AI risks are concentrated in three areas that are difficult to see and harder to manage without a structured approach:

Decentralized, Ungoverned Usage

  • AI risks are not well known or understood across the firm
  • Usage is decentralized and difficult for security teams to monitor
  • Employees may unknowingly input confidential fund data, LP information, or proprietary strategies into external platforms
  • Governance frameworks have not kept pace with adoption speed

Permissioning & Data Exposure

  • Sensitive information is now incredibly easy to surface if user permissioning is not configured correctly
  • Enterprise AI tools connected to internal data sources (SharePoint, email, documents) can expose data across the firm
  • Default settings on most platforms are not the most secure configuration
  • Security teams lack visibility into how AI is being used day to day
Example: What imperfect permissioning looks like in practice

An employee opens an AI tool connected to internal systems and types: "Search SharePoint and find me any information on company compensation." With poorly scoped permissions, they receive documents they were never intended to see. This is not a theoretical scenario — it is a common finding.

AI Works Both Ways — The Threat Actor Angle

The same capabilities that make AI valuable to your clients also make it valuable to threat actors. This is a critical and often overlooked dimension of the conversation.

How threat actors are using AI against your clients

AI enables threat actors to scale phishing, malware, and social engineering at a level that was previously impossible. Campaigns that once required significant manual effort can now be generated, personalized, and deployed in minutes. But the more significant risk is what happens once a threat actor is already inside the environment.

Real-World Scenario
Once inside a firm's environment, a threat actor with access to an AI tool can prompt: "I want to send a wire request for a capital call. Can you find the contact details for firms where I have recent email communication using the words 'capital call,' pull all relevant client information, and draft an email in my normal tone of voice requesting they wire funds to this account?" — The AI does it, accurately, instantly, and in a way that is nearly indistinguishable from a legitimate request.

If threat actors gain access to your client's environment, AI enables them to:

  • Search huge quantities of internal data for sensitive information in seconds
  • Draft convincing emails using the firm's own context — tone of voice, client names, transaction history, proprietary details
  • Identify and target the most valuable relationships and transactions within the firm
  • Execute highly targeted fraud at a scale that was previously manual and time-consuming

Real-World Findings — What We Actually See

Clients are consistently surprised by what's turned on by default. These are representative findings from actual assessments.

HIGH

SSO Not Configured (ChatGPT)

Without SSO, user access may persist after employee termination, credentials may be weak or reused, and access cannot be centrally revoked. This increases the risk of unauthorized access to sensitive prompts, files, and GPT configurations.

Fix: Enforce SSO through the corporate IdP, disable local ChatGPT passwords.

HIGH

Users Can Access External GPTs (ChatGPT)

If users can access GPTs created outside the organization's workspace, they may interact with tools that have unvetted prompts, external API connections, or unknown data handling practices. Sensitive information may be shared with third parties without the firm knowing.

Fix: Restrict users to internally approved GPTs only. Implement a formal review process for any external GPT.

HIGH

Claude Code Bypass Permissions Allowed (Claude)

Bypass mode enables Claude to execute arbitrary commands without oversight. Prompt injection attacks could cause Claude to exfiltrate data, modify critical files, or corrupt system state — without user knowledge.

Fix: Never enable bypass mode in production. Limit to sandboxed dev environments only.

HIGH

Organization Creation Unrestricted (Claude)

Without restrictions, employees can create shadow organizations outside IT visibility and security controls. Shadow organizations bypass corporate policies, audit logging, and data governance requirements entirely.

Fix: Enable organization creation restrictions for all verified domains.

MODERATE

SCIM Not Enabled (ChatGPT)

Manual user management results in orphaned accounts, delayed deprovisioning, and inconsistent role assignments. Former employees retaining access pose a significant insider risk — especially in a firm handling sensitive fund data.

Fix: Enable SCIM provisioning so user lifecycle events automatically reflect in ChatGPT.

MODERATE

Connector Chat Sharing Enabled (Claude)

Sharing connector-based chats could expose derived insights from sensitive data sources even if raw data isn't visible. Recipients may be able to reverse-engineer sensitive information from Claude's responses about restricted data sources.

Fix: Disable connector chat sharing unless absolutely necessary. Require data owner approval.

Key Terms
Shadow AI
Unsanctioned use of AI tools by employees without organizational approval or oversight — a near-universal finding in assessments.
SSO (Single Sign-On)
Centralized authentication that ties AI platform access to corporate identity management, enabling instant deprovisioning when employees leave.
SCIM
System for Cross-domain Identity Management — automates user provisioning and deprovisioning so AI access stays in sync with HR records.
GPT (Custom GPT)
A custom version of ChatGPT configured with specific instructions, files, and capabilities. External GPTs can expose sensitive firm data to unvetted third parties.
Claude Code / Cowork
Advanced Claude features that can access local files and execute code — powerful capabilities that require strict governance to prevent unintended data exposure.
Connector
An integration that connects an AI platform to external data sources (e.g., SharePoint, email). Connectors significantly expand the AI's access to sensitive firm data — and require tight permissioning.
